// Engineering Methodology

How I Think

Backend engineering isn't just about making things work — it's about making them work right. I approach every problem with a structured methodology that emphasizes clarity, performance, and maintainability. Here's how I tackle challenges:

Breaking down complexity

Big systems are made of small, well-defined pieces. I decompose problems into modules, identify dependencies, and design interfaces that are clear and maintainable. This makes everything easier to test, debug, and scale.

Analyzing requirements and constraints

Before writing code, I break down the problem. I map data flows, identify edge cases, and challenge assumptions. This upfront thinking prevents costly rewrites and ensures solutions fit real-world constraints — performance, scale, and maintainability included.

Debugging and optimization

Bugs are inevitable — slow responses shouldn't be. I approach debugging methodically: reproduce, isolate, fix, verify. For performance, I profile before optimizing, focusing on query efficiency, session management, and caching where they matter most.
